DISCOVERING DESIGN INSPIRATIONS

One of the most difficult things as a visual designer is understanding your design style. We often run
into creative slumps that prevent our creativity to flourish. During those moments, the best thing to do
is to refer to other people's work for inspiration. However, it is also important to pay attention to your
own style and find inspiration that will move it in the right direction. I've been a visual designer for 5
years. During those years, I put a lot of effort into exploring visual styles and techniques. I realized that
my design style is catered to minimalism and typography. I work a lot with a monochromatic color
palette to visualize my imagery. Creating sleek and clean work is something I thoroughly enjoy doing.
So when I experience a creative slump, I am researching other minimalist work to create a spark in me.


GUNMAD is a design firm based in London. Their work heavily incoporates
typography and minimalism. I discovered GUNMAD's work a year ago,
while researching for inspiration. Their work really resonates with me and
I observe the types of techniques they use to create their designs. What
stuck out to me, was that most of their work always had black and white.
Although, color has been an important element in design. A lot of people
overlook the fact that good design is one that does not need color to
attract attention. Color is just a bonus!

The difference between being "inspired" and "copying" is also important
to take note of. We want to respect the designers involved while
incorporating our own ideas. Of course, a lot of designs and artwork have
been done before that our individual work is not as "unique" as we deem
it to be. However, we want to stay honest and dig deeper into our own
creativity to create even better designs.
